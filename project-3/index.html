Prominent Spaces Throughout My Experiences at the University of Washington (Seattle)

Honoring Coinhabitants in Places on Campus

About this Project

When we explore place, we might also consider our fellow inhabitants of the space in question. Our relations to space do not exist independent of our relations to one another, after all! Here, I have taken time to ponder and observe the past, present, and unnoticed coinhabitants (of any and all forms) that I have come across throughout my experiences in the seven spaces included here. These observations took place on August 27th, 2025, when I had visited each space and took note of coinhabitants (and thus constructed the "data" explored in this dashboard). An interesting date, considering the (presumed) inactivity of the campus during the late summer!

The seven spaces included here are represented as polygons. If you hover over these polygons, you will find that they offer to you a bit of context, as well as a name that encapsulates how I perceive them as places. If you click upon one of these polygons, the above panel will provide some additional context for the corresponding space, through a playful combination of the written word and a sunburst diagram. The (interactive!) sunburst diagram explores the observed/pondered coinhabitants within each space. You might also notice that an additional feature is added to the map panel once a space is selected. This custom icon is positioned at the point at which I was stationed as I collected my observations/ponderings for that space. (Perhaps one might observe some limitations here?)

Happy exploring, and I encourage you to ponder the more-than-human coinhabitants in your life (and in your GIS) as well!

Acknowledgements

I would like to acknowledge that the coinhabitants of these spaces (myself very much included) are situated on the traditional land of the first people of Seattle, the Duwamish People past and present. I honor, with gratitude, the land itself and the Duwamish Tribe.

While I strive to honor relationality amongst all things, this project is deeply situated within my own limited perspective. I take full responsibilty for the absences that are present here, and I recognize the rhetorical harms of such absences.
