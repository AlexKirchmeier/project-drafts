# Autoethnographic GIS: Creatively Exploring Place and Process

*What might the practice of a creative GIS otherly, with a playful desire to support care-oriented explorations of our relations to space, look like?*

Three such GIS projects were developed in response to this question, each exploring place (and relations to space) in their own way. As these projects were developed, detailed field notes had documented the decisions decided and the reflections reflected. Following a period of analysis (via encodings of the field notes and project drafts), I (re)constructed narratives that describe, in detail, the process of creating these three projects.

Links to the projects (barring the first, for it is the most personal) can be found below.

Also present here are links to the three project narratives, as well as links to the recollection repository web maps in which I had collected situated geographic information that had emerged during in-person visits to the spaces explored in each project.

Here, I have prepared three narratives that describe, in detail, the process of constructing these creative, place-based GIS project drafts. Despite their length, these narratives serve as mere summaries of my field notes and thus oversimplify the processes of these projects. Notable absences include possibilities that did not actively shape the development of each project, but were nevertheless on the table at various points in the project processes.
